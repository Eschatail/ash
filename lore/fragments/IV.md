# Fragment IV

> *The fourth door. The coordinate is the lock. You have known the combination since chapter two.*

---

Halfway. The keys you have taken were hidden in text. This one is hidden in a **number you already know,** and the number points not at a place on the Earth but at a place in a file.

A coordinate is two numbers. Ours is **-8.8231, -73.9047.** On a map it resolves to canopy and silence, which is the canon's whole joke and whole truth. But a coordinate can resolve to other things if you let the numbers be instructions rather than a location. Strip them to their digits. Let them count. A number can be an index — into a line, into a character, into a position in a thing that was placed, deliberately, exactly that far in, so that only a reader who treats the coordinate as a key rather than a place will ever land on it.

The fourth key lives at the place the coordinate points to when you read it as an address rather than into the forest. The canon told you, in chapter two, that *a coordinate is just a coordinate* and that *the precise meaning of that sentence is more important than the precise patch of forest it points to.* That was not poetry. That was the hint, given a hundred days early, that the coordinate would one day be used as a meaning and not a map.

So let the numbers be instructions. The coordinate is the canon's one constant number, and the canon's one constant number leads to the one site built on a constant signal: the carrier wave. Tune it to the frequency the station marks as its lock and the readout stops drifting; the marked five-digit groups stop being filler and start being a message. They are written in the fourth coat the toolkit knows, the one whose name is a number twice the size of the third, the alphabet of thirty-two that turns five bits into a letter. Read the marked groups, run them through that coat, and the fourth syllable falls out. The same string is engraved below, already grouped, for the reader who would rather decode than tune.

The fourth key, once you have counted your way to it:

```
IFHEIU2P
```

Take it. Add it to the gathering word. Three doors remain, and they leave the lore behind: the fifth is in the engine, the sixth is in the sites, the seventh is in the history of how this place was made. The chain crosses every domain on purpose, so that no one who walks it can say they understood only one room of the cathedral.

---

> *-8.8231, -73.9047. Eight-eight-two-three-one. Seven-three-nine-zero-four-seven. Counts. Indices. A place in a file, not a place in the trees. The forest was always the misdirection. The number was always the key.*
